Midnight Sun — 7-Day Stockholm Archipelago Sailing Chart (8–15 August 2026)

Interactive nautical navigation chart for a seven-day clockwise sailing circuit of the Stockholm archipelago in August 2026. Starts and ends at Wasahamnen, Djurgården (Stockholm) and overnights at Vaxholm, Grinda, Möja, Sandhamn, Utö, and Dalarö. Total distance 113.7 nautical miles on OSM-verified fairways with live tide, wind, water-temperature, and wave-height data from SMHI and Open-Meteo. Includes a draggable A→B Quick Nav for ad-hoc bearing & distance, and a full route editor with save / export / clear / reset.

  • Day 1: Wasahamnen → Vaxholm (10.7 nm)
  • Day 2: Vaxholm → Grinda (11.1 nm) via Oxdjupet south circuit past Fredriksborg Fortress (direct mooring) & Archipelago Sauna POIs
  • Day 3: Grinda → Möja (14.9 nm)
  • Day 4: Möja → Sandhamn (9.2 nm)
  • Day 5: Sandhamn → Utö (34.1 nm) via Krokholmssund, Stångsundet, Kymmendösund
  • Day 6: Utö → Dalarö (15.0 nm) via Mysingen & Jungfrufjärden
  • Day 7: Dalarö → Wasahamnen (18.7 nm) via Baggensfjärden & Saltsjön

Midnight Sun is an open, single-file HTML navigation chart for the Stockholm archipelago designed for an August 2026 sailing charter. It uses Leaflet for the slippy-map, ESRI Ocean Base + OpenStreetMap + OpenSeaMap tiles for the chart background, SMHI Open Data for live sea level, waves and water temperature, Open-Meteo for 7-day wind forecasts, and OSM seamark:type=fairway data fetched via the Overpass API for the default water-only routing.

Topics covered: Stockholm archipelago sailing, Skärgården navigation, Vaxholm, Grinda, Möja, Sandhamn, Utö, Dalarö, Fredriksborg Fortress, Archipelago Sauna at Gallnö, Baltic Sea route planning, marine fairways, SMHI havsvattenstånd, Open-Meteo wind forecast, draggable bearing & distance tool, editable Leaflet polyline route, localStorage route persistence, Schema.org TouristTrip itinerary data.
